<p><p>The photovoltaics market is divided into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic photovoltaics utilize carbon-based materials to convert sunlight into electricity, offering flexibility and potential for low production costs. However, they often have lower efficiency and shorter lifespans. In contrast, inorganic photovoltaics, primarily based on silicon, dominate the market due to their higher efficiency, durability, and established manufacturing processes. Each type serves distinct applications, catering to varying demands within the renewable energy sector.</p></p>
